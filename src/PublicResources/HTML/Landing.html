PoolsForCars

Shared rides without the weekly scramble.

PoolsForCars helps students and drivers coordinate school transport by turning recurring travel needs into clear carpooling preferences.

What the pages are for

Each page handles a different part of planning, updating, and finding shared rides.

01

Profile

Use the profile page to manage personal information and your normal Monday to Friday carpooling pattern.

02

Calendar

Use the calendar page when one selected week or weekday needs to be changed, cancelled, or booked separately.

03

Group

Use the group page for an overview of your carpooling groups including the related carpooling information for the groups active day and group member details.

What is the weekly carpooling preferences?

The app uses your carpooling information to understand when you travel, what role you have, and whether a shared route is realistic.

Carpooling availability

Marks whether you are available for carpooling on a weekday.

Passenger or driver

Shows whether you need a seat or can offer seats to others.

Seats available

Tells passengers how many people a driver can bring.

Pickup point

Sets where the ride starts. For safety, do not use your home address. Pick a public place 5-10 minutes away and close to a main road.

Destination

Shows where the ride should end, so routes can be compared.

Arrival time

Helps match people who need to arrive at similar times.

A&Q
What should I use the profile week view for?

Use it for your regular weekly pattern, such as your usual role, pickup point, destination, seats, and arrival time.

What should I use the calendar week view for?

Use it for a specific week or day that differs from your normal profile preferences.

Should I enter my home address?

No. Choose a public pickup point 5-10 minutes away from home and close to a main road.

How does the app support matching?

The app compares users by weekday, role, seats, pickup point, destination, and arrival time. It also looks at how much extra time and distance a pickup would add to a route, then ranks people higher when their travel plans fit together across the week.
